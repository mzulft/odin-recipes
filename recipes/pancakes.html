Banana Pancakes:

Description:

Starting off with the recipe for this delicious food, we need to collect a bit of ingredients. First off, we need flour. Im sure that was pretty apparent since we're making pancakes but if this is your first time, any all-purpose flour regardless of the name brand will work. Next thing we need is sugar. As for the flower, if youre not a huge baker thats the ingredient i would've expected most to not have, but as for sugar, its more commonly used so most should have that on standby. Any sugar will work but white granulated sugar is preferred. The reason being granulated and not powedered is because it creates a tender texture for the pancakes. Some other ingredients that're going to required are going to be: baking powder, any is fine and it gives the pancakes the fluffiness. Salt, white preferably, which adds subtle depth to sugar infused dishes. An egg, which is needed for binding to provide structure, also adds a lot of flavor as well with the yolk. Milk which is going to be used to dissolve some of the dry ingredients we mentioned earlier, also noting that it will provide flavor as well. Second to last ingredient which is needed, is going to be vegetable oil, the reason being is because its harder to burn than butter, and since were going to be putting the batter on the pan waiting for one side to cook, it keeps the pancakes moist and prevents pan sticking. The last, but certainly the most notable ingredient we're going to be utilizing, are the bananas. The base, and what makes banana pancakes, banana pancakes, are going to add so much flavor and texture to this dish, not even only in terms of flavor but in terms of the aesthetics as well. Those are all the ingredients which are going to be needed to craft this recipe. Now lets get onto how we're going to infuse all these together for the final result.

Starting off, we actually want to combine all the ingredients specified earlier, but not just in any order or all of them in a singular bowl, we actually want to mix some ingredients with others and vice versa. You're going to want to get a dedicated bowl for the dry ingredients, so ingredients such as: the flour, sugar, salt, and the baking powder. You're then going to want to put all the ingredients in that bowl and combine them. As for the other bowl, the ingredients you didnt use or the wet ingredients are the ones that're going to be going inside there, so ingredients such as: egg, milk, vegetable oil, and the mashed bananas. Once they're inside a bowl thats big enough to mix so the ingredients dont splash out when you're trying to mix, you're then gonna want to mix the ingredients in the wet bowl until they've all been infused, once you're done with that, if you didn't mix the dry ingredients before you're free to do it now. Once you've mixed both the dry and the wet bowl, youre going to want to add all the contents from the dry bowl to the wet bowl, so transfer it slowly, and once the majority is in you're going to now mix the wet bowl which should contain the contents of both the dry and the wet bowl. If after done mixing the mix is a bit lumpy thats OK, nothing was done wrong and we will proceed to the next step. Since we have our batter prepared the next step is going to be cooking and the creation of the pancakes. What you're going to want to do is, pour the batter in 1/4th cup portions onto a lightly oiled pan. If you have measuring cups that'd be advised but for the most part you'd be able to eyeball it. The heat should be on medium-high heat. We dont want the heat too low or it'll take forever to cook, and we dont want it too high because we potentially risk burning the pancakes, nice and slow. Cook one side for a few minutes then flip with a spatula, do the same for the other side then once both sides are golden brown or until your preferred crispiness, take the pancake off the pan and you've successfully crafted a banana pancake. Now from this point on its good to eat alone, or you can add your preferred toppings, such as syrup or additonal bananas on top of the pancake to eat alongside. Whatever you prefer, they're yours to enjoy, so good job on making it this far.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp white sugar
  • 2 tsps baking powder
  • 1/4th tsp salt
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 tbsps vegetable oil
  • 2 ripe bananas, mashed

Steps:

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Combine flour, white sugar, baking powder, and salt in a bowl. Mix together egg, milk, vegetable oil, and bananas in a second bowl.
  3. Stir flour mixture into banana mixture; batter will be slightly lumpy.
  4. Heat a lightly oiled griddle or frying pan over medium high heat. Pour or scoop the batter onto the griddle, using approximately 1/4 cup for each pancake.
  5. Cook until pancakes are golden brown, 3 to 5 minutes per side. Serve hot.
  6. Serve hot and enjoy!
